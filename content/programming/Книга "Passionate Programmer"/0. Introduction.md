## Сравнительный Анализ
В изначальном варианте данная книга символизирует собой именно изложение опыта, результатов борьбы за свое место в IT, автора оригинала, Чеда Фаулера. Мы же в переводе, уже сквозь года, находясь, можно сказать, в мире, в котором об IT не думает только, может быть, лишь старый мамин утюг не можем не смотреть на данный материал скептически. 

Хоть и можно пропитаться "вайбом" книги, но применить этот запал, который несет в себе текст в современном IT контексте уже, будто бы, крайне сложно. Однако нельзя не согласиться, что данный текст, хоть и отстает по своему смыслу от времени на добрых 15 лет, однако для русскоговорящей аудитории он всё равно остается новым и не прочитанным. По этому, опять же, не смотря на взбалмошную обложку, стоит попытаться взглянуть на него в добром и положительном ключе. 

## Key concepts

### Foreword
David Heinemeier Hansson 37-signals/Ruby on Rails: 

 > To Become remarkable, you have to believe that you're making a significant dent in the universe.
 
 > When I didn't feel like I was having an impact during office hours, it was that much harder to muster the effort to have an impact afterward
 
### Introduction
Chad Fowler: 
#### pt.1
> Strangely, we don't all set out on the quest to lead remarkable lives when we start our careers. 

Author is saying that going with the flow, is not always the best approach. Building the goals and making your own steps towards —  is the strategy which brings us to the fulfillment and joy in our live.

 >If your life is primarily consumed by your work, then loving your work is one of the most important keys to loving your life.
 >Doing your job well means that the activity you do for 50 percent of your available time is something you're good at.

 Here Author is trying to bring the idea, that day after day, by having a success during your activities, you will get your part of joy. This is the key for the hapinnes in everyday life and motivation during your job. 

Author is talking about visiting a meetup with lots of important speakers, and also a like-minded audience.
>Being in that room with those people actually made me a lot smarter. The only way to find yourself around those people is to become one of those people.

The author bring the words about the mindset, which brings you on top of other programmers on a long distance:
>a person who wants to be great is far more likely to become great than someone who just wants to do their job

#### pt.2
> Software is a business

Chad main concern here is that companies would not employ developers because of the love towards them. It is all about money, and the money here rules everything. If you are good and performant you are going to be hired cause you are profitable.

And offers to sell, but not yourself as a human or as a brain. But to sell the knowledge-system you keep building up.

>Think of your career at if it is the life cycle of a product that you are creating& 
> - __Choose your market:__ Pick the tech and the business domains you focus on consciously and deliberately.
> - __Invest in you "product":__ Your knowledge and skills are the cornerstone of your product. Properly investing in them is a critical part of making yourself marketable.
> - __Execute!__ How do you keep up the delivery pace without driving yourself into the dirt?
> - __Market!__ How do you get find recognition in both your company and the industry as a whole without "sucking up"?


#### pt.3 
> The goal of this book is to give you a systematic way of building a remarkable career in software development.

>Stay focused on winning and the fear of losing will be forever a memory.

#### Summary
This book is going to be a great inspirational and informational source for building the motivational and knowledge system for a reader,  based on what, the reader can build up his skillset and develop his spheres of the interest in the IT world. 

However this book also allows the person to pack all his skills and inspirational ideas into, some sort of a product on the HR market. So such a person can build some sort of personal mind-space, which could inspire him for a right actions, to supply him as a professional with a  high demand among IT companies.
